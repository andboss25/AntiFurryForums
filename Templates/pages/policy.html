Privacy Policy for Anti-furry forums

Last updated: 26th of July 2025

Thank you for using Anti-furry forums (“we,” “us,” or “our”). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect your information when you use Anti-furry forums hosted on our designated domain(s). This policy only applies to instances of Anti-furry forums operated and hosted by us on domains set by us (forums.af-comms.net or any subdomains of af-comms.net). If you use or run Anti-furry forums software on other domains or servers, this policy does not apply to those instances.

1. Information We Collect

  • Personal Information: We collect your username, email address, password (hashed), and IP address when you create an account or log in.
  • Activity Data: We collect information about your activity on the app, including posts and interactions.
  • Automatically Collected Data: We use strictly necessary session cookies that enable the app to function properly (such as maintaining your login session). We do not use cookies for tracking, analytics, or advertising purposes at this time. We may collect device information and analytics data in the future, subject to updates to this policy.
  • CAPTCHA Data: We use Google reCAPTCHA to verify whether interactions are performed by humans. Google may collect information such as IP address, browser configuration, mouse movements, and behavioral data. This data is subject to Google’s Privacy Policy and Terms of Service.

2. Account Requirement

You must create an account and log in to use Anti-furry forums hosted on our domain(s).

3. Use of Your Information

  • Provide and maintain the app’s functionality.
  • Send account-related notifications such as verification emails or password reset links.
  • Personalize content and improve user experience.
  • Distribute usernames and related data at our discretion.
  • Monitor and analyze user activity for security purposes, such as detecting and preventing abuse, fraud, or unauthorized access. This includes the use of CAPTCHA to prevent spam or automated bots.

4. Data Sharing

Currently, we do not share your personal data (including your email address) with third parties. However, we reserve the right to share data in the future as we see fit, and will update this policy accordingly.

5. Data Storage

Your data is stored on our own servers or those of third-party providers. The exact location of these servers may vary.

6. Data Security

  • Passwords are encrypted using secure hashing methods.
  • Email addresses are stored securely but not encrypted end-to-end.
  • Other data such as IP addresses and activity logs are not encrypted.
  • Moderators, admins, and owners have access to view usernames, email addresses, IP addresses, and posted content.
  • We implement access controls but do not conduct regular security audits.

7. User Rights

  • You may delete your account or data by yourself if the option is available; otherwise, you can contact us to request deletion at afcommsnet.contact@gmail.com by providing your account username or post ID and context for us to know that you wish to delete the specified content.
  • You may update your account information, including your email address by contacting us at afcommsnet.contact@gmail.com and stating your intentions and account details. This also applies to updating or removing posts and comments.

8. Consent and Opt-Out

Using Anti-furry forums means you agree to our data collection policies. There is no option to opt out of data collection or marketing communications.

9. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We only use strictly necessary session cookies that are essential for the app’s functionality (such as maintaining login sessions). These do not require explicit consent under GDPR, but you are informed here for transparency. We currently do not use cookies or tracking technologies for analytics or advertising.

10. Age Restrictions

There are no age restrictions for using Anti-furry forums.

11. Legal Compliance

We strive to comply with applicable privacy laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) in the EU and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) in the US, where applicable.

12. CAPTCHA Use and Google Services

We use Google reCAPTCHA to help detect and prevent bots from using or abusing the platform. This service may analyze your interactions, IP address, device behavior, and more to determine whether you're human. By using our service, you acknowledge and agree to Google’s Privacy Policy and Terms of Service.
